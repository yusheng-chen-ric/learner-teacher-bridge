William Shakespeare, often regarded as the greatest playwright in the English language, was a master of both tragedy and comedy. Born in 1564 in Stratford-upon-Avon, his works have transcended time, captivating audiences for centuries with their profound exploration of human nature, love, ambition, and betrayal. His plays, such as "Hamlet," "Romeo and Juliet," and "Macbeth," are celebrated for their intricate characters, poetic language, and timeless themes. Beyond the stage, Shakespeare's sonnets reveal a deep understanding of emotion and beauty, solidifying his legacy as a literary genius whose influence continues to shape literature and the arts worldwide.
